Responses Options

The impacts of the United States' global climate change are already being felt and are projected to intensify in the future, particularly without further action to reduce climate-related risks. As climate change 's impacts grow, Americans are facing decisions about how to react.

Adaptation is known as action to prepare and adapt to changing climate conditions — thus reducing negative impacts or taking advantage of new opportunities. The other major category of response options — known as mitigation — involves efforts to reduce the amount and velocity of future climate change by reducing emissions or removing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere.Adaptation and mitigation activities are related in several ways and can be seen as complementary strategies — mitigation measures may mitigate potential risks, whereas adaptation will alleviate the impacts of changes already occurring as a consequence of past and current pollution.
